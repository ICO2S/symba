Introduction to SyMBA

The Centre for Integrated Systems Biology of Ageing and Nutrition (CISBAN) has developed a Data Archive and Integrator (SyMBA) that is based on the FuGE Object Model (FuGE-OM) and which archives, stores, and retrieves raw high-throughput data. Until now, few published systems have successfully integrated multiple omics investigation detailss and information about experiments in a single database. SyMBA is the first published implementation of FuGE that includes a database back-end, expert and standard interfaces, and utilizes a Life Science Identifier (LSID) Resolution and Assigning service to identify objects and provide programmatic access to the database. Having a central data repository prevents deletion, loss, or accidental modification of primary data, while giving convenient access to the data for publication and analysis. It also provides a central location for storage of metadata for the high-throughput data sets, and will facilitate subsequent data integration strategies.

Logging In

By restricting access to SyMBA, we ensure that our data is kept private until we are ready to release it. Further, once SyMBA knows who you are, it can easily retrieve experimental information and data sets owned by you, as well as automatically assign your name to data files you have uploaded. Once logged in, a session specific to you is begun and will remain available until you log out or the session times out.

Your Home Page

Once you have successfully logged on, you will be presented with your home page. From this page you can choose to do a variety of tasks, mainly centred around deposition and modification of data files and experimental information. Your choices are:

  • Deposit some Data. By following this link, you will be presented with a short series of web pages that prompt you to deposit your data and add a few relevant bits of information about the data file. Any primary data source as described in the CISBAN Data Policy (username and password required) may be loaded into SyMBA. By storing our primary data here, we can help prevent deletion, loss, or accidental modification of primary data, while giving convenient access to the data for publication and analysis. Further, by providing extra information on experimental conditions and protocols can provide useful when either viewing or searching for specific data files, or when using the data files as input in further research projects.
  • View your saved experiments. This is a convenient shortcut to the most commonly-used search. This link will take you to a view of all the experiments associated to you.
  • How to use this site. A link to this help page.
  • Update your personal details. If you want to either view or modify your personal details, then follow this link.

Viewing and Modifying Your Details

Keeping up-to-date information about each of us is important for our research. Not only should the correct person (or people) be associated with each data file and experiment that they were a part of, but each change to the information about an experiment should be recorded: not just the time the change was made, but also who performed the change. Finally, if we ever need to contact you about your experiment(s), a valid email address, phone number or address is important.

Each text box on this page is pre-filled with the information currently held by SyMBA. In this way, you can examine your information and change only those items that are incorrect or incomplete. If you have not made any changes, pressing "Cancel" will return you to the home page. If you have made changes, pressing "Enter" will store your changes in SyMBA.

Search SyMBA

SyMBA currently has only one built-in search. However, these are simple to implement and more will be added over time. If you have any recommendations or suggestions as to the type of search you would like to perform, please contact us. Please note: experiments that used to be associated with you but which have since been re-assigned to other people will not show up with this search.

What is Considered a SyMBA Experiment?

When you are adding data to SyMBA, you will see "Experiment" used as a container for your data files. But what is an experiment in the context of SyMBA, and how do your data files relate to it? A SyMBA experiment is an overarching investigation that may contain many data files, of multiple investigation detailss. For instance, if you are performing a large-scale experiment that will involve many microarray results as well as colony growth images and 2D-gels, you do not have to create an experiment for each investigation details. Instead, create just one experiment and add as many data files and investigation detailss as required. If you do not see a investigation details that is correct for you, or the investigation details does not provide the correct identifying factors for your experiment (e.g. you are using different strains in your experiment than the ones in SyMBA), then please contact us.

Choosing Between a New or Existing Experiment for Your Data File

Choosing whether the data files you are about to deposit are part of an experiment you have already told SyMBA about, or are part of an experiment not described to SyMBA before is the first step in the process of providing SyMBA with experimental information. Just click on the answer appropriate for your situation. You will be given the opportunity to either create a new experiment or select an existing experiment depending on your answer here. It is important that you associate data files to the correct experiment: not only will it help you organize your data files, it will also help others understand the full development and scope of your experiment. Erroneous associations could lead to lost data files (i.e. you wouldn't know where you put them) or incorrect conclusions about the data. For information and help on what the SyMBA considers an experiment, please visit the "What is considered a SyMBA Experiment?" help section.

If you leave the submission process at any stage before the final confirmation screen, you may loose all your work and have to re-submit. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact us.

Depositing Data Files

The deposition of data files is the third step in the process of providing SyMBA with experimental information. While you are allowed to deposit as many data files as you like at this stage, each data file must be of the same type (e.g. Microarray). If you have multiple files of different investigation detailss, then please go through the data deposition process once per investigation details. This restriction allows only those experimental processes (also known as protocols) to be chosen that are appropriate for the data file during the next stage of the submission process. Additionally, all data files deposited at this stage WILL be associated with the same experiment. Therefore, if you are depositing data files from different experiments, then please go through the data deposition process once per experiment.

Selecting a Protocol for Your Data File

The association of each data file with a protocol is the final step in the process of providing SyMBA with experimental information. Each data file is the result of a measurement of one of your experimental materials. We need to join together each data file with the exact procedure that was used to generate it. On this page is a list of all of the data files you uploaded in the previous page. Each data file name has, next to it, a list of appropriate protocols for the investigation details. These protocols were created in the database with the consultation of the research groups within CISBAN, and are only useful in that context. If your protocol is not present, or if you find an error or lack of important information, then please contact us.

Completion of Submission and Downloading Updated Data File

At this stage, your experimental data will have been stored in the archive. Before you continue, please click on the "Download" button to download your file from the database. This is CISBAN policy, though it may seem counter-intuitive. The reason we require that you download your file is so the CISBAN identifier that has been generated for your data file can be automatically attached. It is very important that you keep this CISBAN identifier with its associated data file. For more information on why this is important, please see the section of this help entitled "What is the Identifier that Gets Assigned to My Data File?". Clicking on the "Download" button will not only start your download, but also return you to SyMBA home page.

What is the Identifier that Gets Assigned to My Data File?

When you upload a data file to SyMBA, in many other areas of the website it is referred to with an identifier that will look something like this:

Gre_MCA_0365

An identifier like this has been created to uniquely identify each one of your archived data files. When you finish the loading process, you will be prompted to download the file you just provided. Doing this means you get back a version of your file with this identifier as the filename. Any time you download one of your data files from SyMBA, it will be named using this identifier. Keep track of this identifier, as it will allow you to absolutely identifiy your data file on your local computer with the one in the database.

This identifier is created in the following manner:

  1. The first 3 characters are the first three characters of your last name.
  2. The next 3 characters (after the first underscore, "_") are a three-letter acronym that stands for the type of data the identifier belongs to. For example, "MCA" means Microarray.
  3. The final 4 characters (after the second underscore) are a set of 4 random digits.
